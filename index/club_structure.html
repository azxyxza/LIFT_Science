Education See more...

Within the Education Department, we establish a vital link between UBC volunteers and Kenya students (secondary and primary school) from the slum regions through WhatsApp and Zoom. Volunteers will support their teacher shortage by teaching science subjects online, including Math, Chemistry, Biology, and Physics. The duties of our volunteers could include:

  • Work as teaching assistants during weekends to help students grasp difficult topics.
  • Collaborate with local teachers to introduce new topics.
  • Prepare students for the nationally significant KCPE/KCSE exams, pivot for getting higher education.
  • Impart more advanced skills like programming.

Funding See more...

We will establish funding objectives for each academic year and organize fundraising events. Upon successful completion of these goals, we are able to provide a lot of practical help to local communities, including but not limited to:

  • Daily meals for students.
  • Access to clean water.
  • Technology (Wi-Fi, laptops, etc.).
  • Libraries and laboratories.
  • Sanitary pads for girls.
  • Teaching Salaries.
  • Enhance the overall quality of life for Albarta School vicinity.
